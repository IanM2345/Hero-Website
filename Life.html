KSI's Life Timeline

Early Life

1993

Olajide Olayinka Williams Olatunji was born on 19 June 1993 in London and was raised in Watford, Hertfordshire.[5][6] His father, Olajide Olatunji Sr., was born in Ibadan, Nigeria. His mother, Yinka Olatunji (née Atinuke), is from Islington, London.[7] He and his parents are from the Yoruba ethnic group. He was educated at Berkhamsted School in Berkhamsted, where he met future collaborator and Sidemen member Simon Minter.

Youtube

2009 to Present

KSI, a YouTuber, started his career in 2008 with the username "JideJunior" and later shifted to vlog-style content. He dropped out of sixth form college to pursue his career, earning around £1,500 a month. Despite controversy, KSI signed with Polaris and joined the British YouTube group, the Sidemen. In 2015, he published a biography and left the Sidemen in 2017. In 2019, he was ranked second in The Sunday Times' top 100 UK influencers and Britain's biggest influencer.

Music

2015 to Present

KSI, a British rapper, began his career in 2011 with comedy-rap songs for his YouTube channel. He released his debut single, "Lamborghini," in 2015, featuring grime MC P Money. He released several EPs, including "Keep Up," "Goes Off," "Friends with Benefits," "Creature," and "Disstracktions." In 2018, he released "Uncontrollable" and "On Point." In 2019, he released a collaborative album with Randolph, New Age, and signed with RBC Records and BMG to further develop his music career. His debut studio album, Dissimulation, was released in May 2020, and it debuted at number two on the UK Albums Chart. KSI also featured on "Lighter" by Nathan Dawe and "Loose" by S1mba. In 2021, he created his own record label, "The Online Takeover," with music manager Mams Taylor. His second album, All Over the Place, debuted at number 1 on the UK Album Charts and was certified gold by the BPI. In 2022, KSI released his fifth single, "Lose," with American rapper Lil Wayne.

Boxing

2017 to Present

KSI, a boxing promoter, has faced numerous challenges in his career, including a feud with British YouTuber Joe Weller, a fight with American YouTuber Logan Paul, and a rematch with Mexican professional boxer Luis Alcaraz Pineda. He has also faced other notable opponents, such as Dillon Danis, FaZe Temperrr, and Joe Fournier. In 2023, KSI faced Tommy Fury in a charity sparring match, which generated 1,300,000 pay-per-view buys. However, KSI's appeal to the Professional Boxing Association (PBA) was rejected, and he is still moving into the second of three possible stages. In December 2023, KSI will face IShowSpeed in a charity sparring match, aiming to boycott his rivals' fight. Despite the challenges, KSI continues to fight and promote boxing events, showcasing his skills and dedication to mental health awareness.

Acting

2015 to 2023

In 2016, KSI starred in the British comedy film Laid in America, directed by Sam Milman and Peter Vass. In 2018, he released a documentary film titled KSI: Can't Lose, detailing his journey to becoming the world's YouTube champion. In 2023, KSI released a documentary called KSI: In Real Life, produced by Amazon Prime Video and Louis Theroux, focusing on his life and career. The film was praised for its wit and brutality.

Prime

2022 to Present

In January 2022, KSI announced that he and Logan Paul had founded a drinks company known as Prime.[140] Prime is a range of sports drinks, drink mixes, and energy drinks created and marketed by Prime Hydration, LLC

Misfits

2022 to Present

On 22 June 2021, KSI announced that he and Wasserman Boxing had partnered up to launch a boxing promotional company called 'Misfits Boxing'.[125][126] The first event was scheduled to take place on 27 August 2022 with KSI also set to make his boxing return as the main event.[127] A series of other boxing events titled "MF & DAZN: X Series" in collaboration with DAZN, who were the distributors for KSI's previous rematch against Logan Paul, was announced, along with events to follow, marking the KSI vs Swarmz and Luis Alcaraz Pineda event as MF & DAZN: X Series 001.[126] On 10 January 2023, it was announced that Misfits Boxing and DAZN had signed a 5 year deal to continue distribute events with two pay-per-view each year
